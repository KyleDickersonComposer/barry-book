### What might be some of the rules?
- Landing with chord tones, or delays of chord tones, on strong beats (root, 3rd, 5th)
- If we have a prominant fourth it's kind of like twoing the five or something (implies a sub though...)
- Elaborations of contrapuntal lines
- There is a looseness that happens when there is a bar or so that doesn't have the anchor of landing a chord tone on a strong beat. (This loosening effect is a thing to look at.)
- Five minor on the dominant
- If a chord happens over two bars, it's quite common to have some color chord in the first bar (We are also seeing this pattern in two bar things of some kind of delay into a standard line in the second bar)
- There are some common patterns where they go some kind of line that has the seventh and third of the predominant chord in the context of a single bar two-five.
- There is something about thinking in terms of reduced lines.
- It's very jagged, up and down line shape.
